🐧 Wayland-native speech-to-text

Press a key. Speak.
Get text.

dictate is a signal-driven CLI that transcribes your voice using Mistral, Groq, or local Whisper — then pipes clean text to stdout.

Install dictate Source
# install
$ curl -fsSL https://dictate.adityamer.dev/install.sh | sh

# use
$ dictate --pipe-to wl-copy
$ pkill --signal SIGUSR1 dictate
How it works

A single UNIX signal controls the entire flow. No daemon polling.

01

Bind a key

Assign a keybind in your Wayland compositor

02

Speak

dictate records from PipeWire with audio feedback

03

Signal

SIGUSR1 stops recording and sends to your provider

04

Get text

Text piped to stdout, clipboard, or direct typing

Built for the terminal

A UNIX citizen. Composable. Zero overhead when idle.

⚡

Signal-driven

SIGUSR1 triggers transcription. No polling, no wasted cycles.

🔧

Pipe anywhere

--pipe-to sends output to wl-copy, ydotool, or any command.

🎵

Audio feedback

Musical beeps confirm recording start, stop, and success.

🌐

Multi-provider

Mistral, Groq, or local Whisper. Switch with one env var.

🏗️

Wayland native

PipeWire capture. Hyprland, Niri, GNOME, KDE, Sway.

🔒

Privacy

Local Whisper — audio never leaves your machine.
